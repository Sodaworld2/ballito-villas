The Wildcard — and maybe the best idea

Prince Albert, Karoo

Instead of cramming 16 people into one big beach house — what if you rented 3 charming houses in a 260-year-old art village at the foot of a UNESCO mountain pass?

Prince Albert is a tiny Karoo village that punches absurdly above its weight. 30+ artist studios. A food scene that makes foodies weep. Victorian houses with stoeps and fig trees. No traffic. No crowds. Kids can walk the streets alone. Mum can sit under a tree with a book. Everyone cooks Karoo lamb together under the Milky Way. December days are 28-30°C, dry, no humidity — no aircon needed.

Things to Do (without trying)

The village IS the activity. But if you need a list...

Swartberg Pass

UNESCO World Heritage gravel pass over the mountains. 27km of jaw-dropping Karoo landscape. Drive it, stop at every viewpoint. The highlight of the trip.

Open Studios Route

30+ artists open their studios to visitors. Printmakers, jewellers, botanical painters, blacksmiths, calligraphers. Walk between them. Gina's paradise.

Stargazing

Zero light pollution. The Milky Way looks like a painting. Bring a blanket, lie in the garden, don't say anything for 20 minutes.

Ghost Walk

Evening guided walk through Prince Albert's haunted spots. The kids will talk about it for months.

Olive & Fig Farms

Visit working olive presses and fig drying racks. Taste oil, cheese, dried fruit. Buy gifts for everyone back home.

Die Hel (Gamkaskloof)

Day trip to the most isolated valley in South Africa. 4x4 only. An adventure for the brave.

Getting There

Prince Albert

From Joburg: 11-12hr drive (just 35 min off the N1 at Three Sisters). Or fly to George (2hrs), drive 134km over the Swartberg Pass.

Vibe of the drive: Once you turn off the N1, the Karoo opens up. Big sky, silence, space. The Swartberg Pass descent is the arrival ceremony.

Ocean Retreat (Westbrook)

From Joburg: 6hr drive on the N3 (or fly to Durban, 45-min drive north).

Mic's traffic advice: Leave before Dec 20 or after Dec 25 to avoid the Joburg-KZN exodus. Half of Joburg drives this road over Christmas.
